What's Changed Since 2018

Economic Valuations

  • VSL increased from $9M to $13.6M (federal standard)
  • Spine injury costs doubled to $94,285
  • General MSD costs up 294% to $47,316

Workforce Growth

  • Interventional cardiologists: +73% (3,255 to 5,639)
  • Electrophysiologists: +37% (1,925 to 2,629)
  • Total at-risk clinicians: +9%

Clinical Trends

  • Radial access rate: 30% to 68% (higher radiation)
  • MSD prevalence: 53% to 66% (no improvement)
  • Cervical injury rate: 10x general population

What's Not Included

  • Cataract costs (progression rates unknown)
  • Cognitive decline impacts
  • Physician replacement/turnover costs
  • Lost procedure revenue

About This Study

This interactive web application updates the 2018 ORSIF Economic Study, which first quantified the economic costs of occupational health hazards in interventional fluoroscopy at approximately $49 million annually in the United States.

The 2025 update incorporates:

  • Latest federal economic valuations (VSL, injury costs)
  • Updated physician workforce data from AAMC and ABIM
  • Recent MSD prevalence data from SCAI 2023 survey
  • Current radiation exposure trends from occupational studies

Note: Some data points are marked as "placeholder" where primary sources were unavailable or require paid subscriptions. These estimates use either 2018 baseline values or publicly available approximations.
